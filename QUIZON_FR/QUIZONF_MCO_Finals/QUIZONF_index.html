Phillippine Folk Dance:

TINIKLING

Definition

A traditional Philippine Folk dance that involves dancers stepping, hopping, and jumping between two rhytmically clapped bamboo poles.

History

Oldest folk dance found in Leyte during the Spanish colonial era. It mimics the movements of the tikling bird, with dancers stepping over and in between over a rhytmic tapping and sliding of bamboo poles.

Originates

The origin of the Tinikling dance is most-likely from the Visyan Islands, inspired by the movements of the tikling bird as it dodged bamboo traps. Urban legends also suggest an origin from a punishment dance during the Spanish colonial era, where people had to jump over bamboo poles being clapped together. The dance is a representation of the agility and resilience of the Filipino people.
