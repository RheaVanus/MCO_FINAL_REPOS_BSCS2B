It evolved from everyday activities into symbolic movements with cultural and spiritual significance, with different regions developing unique traditions around the world. In the Philippines, folk dances combined indigenous customs with Spanish, Chinese, and Islamic influences, and today they continue to thrive in schools and festivals, keeping heritage alive and connecting communities.

Popular Folk Dances

Tinikling

Origin: Leyte, Philippines. Tinikling is a traditional dance involving two people beating, tapping, and sliding bamboo poles while dancers step over and in between them.

Itik-Itik

Origin: Surigao, Philippines. Inspired by duck movements, Itik-Itik mimics the walking and waddling motion of ducks, performed during festive occasions.

Maglalatik

Origin: Laguna, Philippines. This dance depicts a fight over coconut meat, with dancers wearing coconut shells as part of the rhythm and steps.

Kuratsa

Origin: Visayas, Philippines. Kuratsa is a courtship dance performed during celebrations, featuring lively music and flirtatious movements.

Binasuan

Origin: Pangasinan, Philippines. Binasuan showcases dancers balancing full glasses of rice wine on their heads and hands while performing intricate steps.

Singkil

Origin: Mindanao, Philippines. Singkil is a royal dance narrating a legend, where dancers skillfully step between clashing bamboo poles, representing a dramatic story.
