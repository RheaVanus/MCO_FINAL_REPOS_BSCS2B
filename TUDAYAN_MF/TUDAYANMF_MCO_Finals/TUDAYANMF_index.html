World of Waltz

Origins

The Waltz began in 18th‑century Europe, where its flowing steps and close embrace reshaped social dance. It soon became the ballroom’s most iconic symbol of elegance and refinement.

Style

Defined by smooth rise‑and‑fall, graceful turns, and a steady 3/4 rhythm, the Waltz creates the illusion of gliding across the floor with effortless charm.

Exprerience

The Waltz is more than steps, it’s about harmony, connection, and storytelling. Partners move as one, weaving rhythm and emotion into a timeless dance.

Definition

The waltz is one of the most classic and romantic ballroom dances, known for its smooth and graceful movements. Originating in Austria in the 18th century, it quickly became a favorite among European aristocrats. The waltz is danced in 3/4 time with a signature “rise and fall” technique, giving it a floating and effortless feel.
