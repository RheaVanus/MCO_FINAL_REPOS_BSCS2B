JIVE BALLROOM DANCE

The Jive is a lively, energetic, and upbeat ballroom dance belonging to the Latin dance category. It evolved from swing and jitterbug during the 1930s and 1940s, known for its quick footwork, sharp kicks, and playful movements. Jive is danced in 4/4 time with a fast tempo, making it one of the most athletic and fun ballroom dances to learn and perform.

TYPES OF JIVE DANCE

Swing Jive

Swing Jive is a fun, bouncy version of jive influenced by swing and jitterbug. It features kicks, turns, and light footwork that highlight energy and rhythm.

Ballroom Jive

The official competition style, Ballroom Jive uses sharp kicks, quick steps, and precise timing. It is the fastest of all Latin ballroom dances.

Rock & Roll Jive

A jive variation influenced by rock-and-roll culture. It features acrobatic moves, spins, and high-energy footwork.
