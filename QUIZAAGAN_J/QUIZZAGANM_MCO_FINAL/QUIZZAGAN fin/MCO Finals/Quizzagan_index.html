SALSA: Lively and rhythmic Latin partner dance.

Originates

Salsa dance originated in eastern Cuba in the 1900s, evolving from a blend of the Cuban son and Afro-Cuban rumba. It later developed in New York City, where Puerto Rican and Cuban immigrant musicians fused their music and dance styles with American jazz.

History

Salsa dance originated in Cuba and Puerto Rico in the early 20th century, evolving from a fusion of African rhythms and Spanish partner-dance traditions like the Cuban son and Afro-Cuban rumba. The dance gained significant popularity in the United States, particularly in New York City, where Puerto Rican and Cuban musicians and dancers blended it with jazz to create new styles.

Cultural Identity and Heritage

Salsa is a powerful means for Latino communities, especially diaspora populations, to connect with their roots and celebrate their heritage. It serves as a source of pride and a way to pass on traditions to new generations.
