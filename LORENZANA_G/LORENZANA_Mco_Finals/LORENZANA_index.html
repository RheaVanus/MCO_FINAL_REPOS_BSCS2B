Hip-hop

Hip hop is an urban cultural movement and a music genre that originated in the South Bronx, New York City, in the 1970s.

Hip-Hop

It is defined by four main elements: emceeing (rapping), DJing (using turntables), B-boying (breakdancing), and graffiti art.

Hip-Hop

The music genre itself typically involves rapping over a rhythmic beat, while the broader culture includes a distinct style of dance, graffiti, and other forms of expression.

Hip hop originated in the Bronx, New York City, during the early 1970s at block parties, evolving from a subculture with four key elements DJing, MCing (rapping), breakdancing, and graffiti art.
Pioneering DJs like DJ Kool Herc extended percussive breaks to create a continuous danceable rhythm.
The culture became a global movement and a powerful tool for social commentary and expression, moving from its roots in marginalized communities to the mainstream.

Breaking

Breaking, or breakdancing, is an energetic, acrobatic hip hop dance style that originated in the Bronx, New York, in the 1970s.

Locking

Locking is a funk dance style characterized by quickly freezing or "locking" in place during a dance movement, holding the position for a brief moment, and then continuing with the same speed.

Popping

Popping is a style of hip-hop dance that involves quickly contracting and relaxing muscles to create a sudden, jerking "pop" or "hit" in the body.

Funk

Funk dance refers to a group of street dance styles like popping, locking, and boogaloo.

Stepping

Stepping involves extensive and highly syncopated clapping, slapping of hands against various parts of the body, and, of course, the foot stomping

Lyrical

Lyrical in hip hop is a fusion style that blends hip hop's foundational movements with the emotional storytelling and fluidity of lyrical dance.
